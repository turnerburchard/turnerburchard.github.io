About Me

Hello! I'm Turner Burchard, a passionate and driven computer scientist with a strong background in software engineering, research, and education. I thrive on solving complex problems and pushing the boundaries of what technology can achieve, in an environment where I can continue to grow alongside talented developers.

Take a look at my resume or email me for more information.

Professional Background: I'm currently an intern at Quiq, where I'm contributing to the development of a conversational AI backend for a customer support web application. Using Scala and Python, I've had the opportunity to enhance user satisfaction by creating advanced AI chatbots with impressive LLM reasoning capabilities. My role as a full software engineer on a high-achieving team has allowed me to consistently exceed project expectations, with over 200 commits and thousands of lines of code contributed to more than 20 microservices. Before joining Quiq, I gained valuable experience as a Full Stack Engineering Intern at Brightvine, where I worked on the creation of blockchain-based financial web applications from the ground up. In this role, I had the opportunity to work closely with a small development team, utilizing a modern microservices architecture with React frontend and Go backend. In addition to my industry experience, I served as a Teaching Assistant at Montana State University, where I led weekly class sessions and provided tutoring for courses in Honors Chemistry and Data Science with Python. Prior to that, I conducted research as an Undergraduate Research Scientist in the Wiedenheft Lab, where I applied my computational skills to model CRISPR and COVID in Python and Perl. I also had the privilege of interning with D.A. Davidson in IT Risk Management, where I streamlined processes using Python automation, achieving significant efficiency gains. In this role, I managed network proxy, email filters, and vendors for a large finance company.
